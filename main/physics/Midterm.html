No 2 Introduction

What is Kinematics?

Kinematics is a fundamental branch of physics that delves into the motion of objects without delving deeply into the forces behind that motion. It's like studying how things move—whether they're going left or right, up or down, or even simultaneously in multiple directions—without focusing on what makes them move in that way.

Who is Sir Isaac Newton?

Isaac Newton was one of the world's great scientists because he took his ideas, and the ideas of earlier scientists, and combined them into a unified picture of how the universe works. Isaac explained the workings of the universe through mathematics. He formulated laws of motion and gravitation.

No 6 3 Laws

Law of Inertia

The first law states that if a body is at rest or moving at a constant speed in a straight line, it will remain at rest or keep moving in a straight line at constant speed unless it is acted upon by force.

Law of Acceleration

The second law is a quantitative description of the changes that a force can produce on the motion of a body. It states that the time rate change of the momentum of a body is equal in both magnitude and direction to the force imposed on it. The acceleration of the body is directly proportional to the net force acting on the body and inversely proportional to the mass of the body.

Law of Action-Reaction

The third states that when two bodies interact, they apply forces to on another that are equal in magnitude and opposite in direction. The third law is also known as the law of action and reaction.
