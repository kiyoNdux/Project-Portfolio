No 6 Electricity

What is Electricity?

Electricity - is the flow of electrical power or charge. It is a secondary energy source which means that we get it from the conversion of other sources of energy, like coal, natural gas, oil, nuclear power and other natural sources, which are called primary sources.

Types of Electricity

  • Static electricity (Electricity at rest) - occurs when there is an imbalance of positively and negatively charged atoms. Electrons then jump from atom to atom, releasing energy
  • Current electricity (Electricity in motion) – or dynamic electricity, exists when charges are able to constantly flow. Types: direct current (DC) and alternating current (AC).
  • Electric Conductors – elements with high conductivity, which have very mobile electrons. Metals like copper, silver, and gold are usually our top choices for good conductors.
  • Electric Insulators – elements with low conductivity. They prevent the flow of electrons.

How do charges interact with each other?

  • Electrostatic force - or Coulomb force / interaction, is a force that operates between charges.

No 7 Electrostatics

Laws of Electrostatics

  • 1st Coulomb’s Law – two charged particles of same charge (positive or negative) will repel each other and two charged particles of opposite charges (one positive and one negative) will attract each other
  • 2nd Coulomb’s Law – the force between the two particles is: (1) Directly proportional to the product of the magnitude of the charges (2) Inversely proportional to the square of the distance between the two charges.
