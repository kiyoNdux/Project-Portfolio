No 2 Introduction

Conic Sections

It is a curve obtained as the intersection of the surface of a cone with a plane, he types of conic section are:

  • Hyperbola
  • Parabola
  • Elipse
  • Circle

No 3 Introduction to Circle, Elipse, Parabola and Hyperbola

Circle

A circle is formed when the plane is parallel to the base of the cone.

Parabola

A parabola is formed when the plane is parallel to the surface of the cone, resulting in a U-shaped curve that lies on the plane.

Elipse

The ellipse is one of the conic sections, that is produced, when a plane cuts the cone at an angle with the base.

Hyperbola

The hyperbola is one of the conic section formed by the intersection of a plane and a double cone.

No 6 List of What I Learned:

    • Learning Outcomes - Focused on creating visualizations and solving problems related to conic sections, sequences, and mathematical induction using computer applications.
    • Introduction to Conic Sections - Conic sections are curves formed by the intersection of a cone and a plane. They include:
      • Circle - Formed when the plane is parallel to the base of the cone.
      • Parabola - Formed when the plane is parallel to the surface of the cone, resulting in a U-shaped curve.
      • Ellipse - Produced when a plane cuts the cone at an angle to the base.
      • Hyperbola - Formed by the intersection of a plane and a double cone.
    • Properties of Conic Sections - Each conic has specific properties:
      • Circle - Circumference, Center, Radius, and Diameter.
      • Parabola - Vertex, Focus, Focal Length, Latus Rectum, Directrix, Axis.
      • Ellipse - Focus, Center, Major Axis, Minor Axis, Vertex, Co-Vertex.
      • Hyperbola - Foci, Focal Length, Traverse Axis, Conjugate Axis, Axes of Symmetry, Center, Vertices, Semi-Major Axis, Semi-Minor Axis, and Asymptotes.
